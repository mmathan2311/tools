Compute monthly EMI for any loan amount, interest rate and tenure. Includes amortization table and a cumulative principal/interest chart.

Enter your loan amount, annual interest rate and tenure in years. Click Calculate EMI to view the monthly payment amount, total interest payable and a full amortization breakdown. Use the CSV button to export the schedule for record keeping.

What is an EMI Calculator?

An EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) calculator helps you find out exactly how much you need to pay each month for a loan. Whether you are taking a home loan, car loan, personal loan, or education loan, this free tool instantly tells you your monthly payment, total interest payable, and gives you a complete amortization schedule.

EMI Formula

The EMI is calculated using the standard formula: EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n – 1), where P is the principal loan amount, r is the monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the loan tenure in months. Our calculator applies this formula instantly so you get accurate results without doing any manual math.

How to Use This EMI Calculator

Simply enter your loan amount in rupees, the annual interest rate offered by your bank, and the loan tenure in years. Click "Calculate EMI" to see your monthly instalment, total interest amount, and the full repayment schedule broken down month by month. You can also download the amortization table as a CSV file for your records.

Why Use an EMI Calculator Before Taking a Loan?

Knowing your EMI in advance helps you plan your monthly budget effectively. You can compare different loan amounts and tenures to find a combination that suits your income. A longer tenure reduces your EMI but increases total interest paid, while a shorter tenure saves interest but increases monthly burden. Use this tool to find the right balance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does prepaying a loan reduce my EMI?

Yes. When you make a part-prepayment, the outstanding principal reduces. You can either keep the same tenure and lower your EMI, or keep the same EMI and reduce your tenure — saving significant interest either way.

What is the difference between flat rate and reducing balance EMI?

In a flat rate loan, interest is charged on the original principal throughout. In a reducing balance loan (most bank loans), interest is charged only on the outstanding balance, making the effective interest lower. Our calculator uses the reducing balance method.

Is the EMI fixed for the entire loan tenure?

For fixed-rate loans, yes — the EMI stays the same every month. For floating-rate loans, the EMI can change when the RBI repo rate changes, which affects your bank's base rate.

What is an amortization table?

An amortization table shows the break-up of each EMI payment — how much goes towards interest and how much reduces the principal. In the early months, most of the EMI goes to interest. Towards the end, most goes to principal repayment.

Can I use this for a home loan EMI calculation?

Absolutely. Enter the home loan amount (e.g. ₹50,00,000), the interest rate (e.g. 8.5%), and the tenure (e.g. 20 years) to instantly see your monthly home loan EMI and total payment over the loan period.
