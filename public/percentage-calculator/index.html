What is a Percentage Calculator?

A percentage calculator helps you solve the most common percentage problems instantly — finding what percentage one number is of another, calculating a percentage of a number, finding percentage increase or decrease, and more. Percentages are used everywhere: discounts, marks, profit/loss, tax, interest rates, and data analysis.

How to Calculate Percentage

The basic formula is: Percentage = (Part / Whole) × 100. For example, if you scored 450 out of 600, your percentage = (450 / 600) × 100 = 75%. To find a percentage of a number: Value = (Percentage / 100) × Number. For example, 20% of ₹5,000 = (20/100) × 5,000 = ₹1,000.

Common Percentage Use Cases

Percentage calculations come up in everyday life: calculating exam marks and grades, finding discount amounts while shopping, computing profit or loss in business, working out GST and tax amounts, understanding interest rates on loans and savings, and analyzing data in reports. This tool handles all these scenarios instantly.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate percentage increase?

Percentage Increase = ((New Value – Old Value) / Old Value) × 100. For example, if your salary went from ₹30,000 to ₹36,000, the increase = ((36,000 – 30,000) / 30,000) × 100 = 20%.

How do I calculate a discount percentage?

Discount Percentage = ((Original Price – Sale Price) / Original Price) × 100. If an item costs ₹800 and is on sale for ₹600, the discount = ((800 – 600) / 800) × 100 = 25%.

How to find what percentage X is of Y?

Simply divide X by Y and multiply by 100. For example, 25 is what percent of 200? = (25 / 200) × 100 = 12.5%.
