What is a Word Counter?

A word counter is a free online tool that instantly counts the number of words, characters, sentences, paragraphs, and estimates reading time in any text you paste or type. It is an essential tool for writers, bloggers, students, journalists, SEO professionals, and anyone who needs to meet specific word count requirements.

Why Word Count Matters

Different writing contexts have specific word count requirements: blog posts typically need 1,000–2,000 words for good SEO, academic essays have strict word limits, Twitter allows 280 characters per tweet, LinkedIn articles perform best at 1,500–2,000 words, and résumés should ideally be under 700 words. This tool helps you hit the right length every time.

Reading Time Estimation

The average adult reading speed is 200–250 words per minute. Based on your word count, this tool estimates how long it will take an average reader to finish your content. This is useful for web content, presentations, speeches, and YouTube script writing where timing matters.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does this tool count words in Indian languages?

The word counter works with any Unicode text including Tamil, Hindi, Telugu, Kannada, Malayalam, and other Indian languages. Words are counted by whitespace-separated units, which works consistently across languages.

What is the ideal word count for an SEO blog post?

For competitive keywords, aim for 1,500–2,500 words. For informational content, 1,000–1,500 words is sufficient. Longer content tends to rank better as it covers topics more comprehensively, but quality and relevance matter more than length alone.

How many words is a 5-minute speech?

At an average speaking pace of 130–150 words per minute, a 5-minute speech is approximately 650–750 words. For a faster pace (180 wpm), it would be around 900 words. Use this tool to calibrate your speech to the right length.
